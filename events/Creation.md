Creation describes the biblical account for creating the heavens and the earth. According to the Genesis story, God created the heavens and the earth, including its creatures, out of a void ([Genesis 1:2](https://esv.org/Genesis+1:2)) over a period of six days ([Genesis 1:3-31](https://esv.org/Genesis+1:3-31)), and He rested on the seventh day ([Genesis 2:1-3](https://esv.org/Genesis+2:1-3)).

## The Days of Creation

According to the historical narrative found in Genesis, God performed the following creative works:

**Day 1:** God created light. He named the light "_Day_", and the darkness He called "_Night_" ([Genesis 1:3-5](https://esv.org/Genesis+1:3-5)).

**Day 2:** God created a separation between the water on the surface and the water above it. He called the separation, or expanse, "_Heaven_" ([Genesis 1:6-8](https://esv.org/Genesis+1:6-8)).

**Day 3:** God gathered the waters together and created dry ground. The dry ground he called "_Earth_" or "_land_", and the gathered water God called "_Seas_" ([Genesis 1:9-10](https://esv.org/Genesis+1:9-10)). God also created various vegetation on the Earth ([Genesis 1:11-13](https://esv.org/Genesis+1:11-13)).

**Day 4:** God created the Sun, the Moon, and all the stars in the sky to separate day from night, and be for signs and seasons, and days and years. They were to rule over the day and the night, and separate light from darkness ([Genesis 1:14-19](https://esv.org/Genesis+1:14-19)).

**Day 5:** God created the animals of the seas and the birds of the air. He commanded them to multiply throughout the seas and the earth, respectively ([Genesis 1:20-23](https://esv.org/Genesis+1:20-23)).

**Day 6:** God created the beasts on the land and mankind. It was on this day that God also gave mankind dominion over His creation. Mankind was tasked to take care of it and subdue it&mdash;maintain control and order ([Genesis 1:24-31](https://esv.org/Genesis+1:24-31)).

**Day 7:** Though no work was performed, day 7 is considered part of the creation narrative, and it reveals to us that God rested. As such, this day is considered holy, and believers are commanded to honor it as such ([Genesis 2:1-3](https://esv.org/Genesis+2:1-3)).
